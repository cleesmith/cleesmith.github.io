Eating always beats Exercising

Never forget that you can not out exercise what you eat. Well, you can, if you try hard enough, but you will die trying. After all, a handful of nuts (about 15) is the equivalent of running a mile or walking two miles. You can literally chew and swallow 15 nuts in a few seconds, but you will be walking that off for 45 minutes.

When it comes to calories, life is not fair. Unless you have a math jones, stop counting so much and focus on the quality of what you eat and eat less of it.

Never turn to the dark side

Do not eat food that's comes in a bag, box, or can -- most of it is not really food.
There's probably a good, as in bad, reason it is stored in the dark.

If it grew under ground (dark) then there are way too many carbs in it, so don't eat it. For example -- carrots, potatoes, and peanuts -- these are roots and one of their main purpose is to be a dense energy store for plants. Roots are the beer belly of a plant, and if you eat too many you will have a beer belly too.

Respect yourself

Eating more than 30 grams of carbohydrates is not necessary. Our bodies make more than enough sugars (carbs) already, often times, too much.

Treat food like an intimate relationship, and don't just allow anything inside of you. Respect your mouth as much as your other orifices.

Let food be thy medicine and medicine be thy food.
Hippocrates


First Fast

After fasting for 40 hours I lost two pounds. That's impressive, and it really wasn't that hard. Of course, the thing about weight loss is keeping it off. Alas, I did eat too much after breaking the fast, which is only natural I think.

It helped to distract myself by going on a day trip. So I visited Boxerwood in Lexington, Virginia. It's a free arboretum to walk and play around in, which was once the home of a local doctor (MD) who had a fondness for growing trees. Mostly it seems aimed at city kid's education about nature in a fairie tale like setting. But I found it interesting and lingered for a few hours as the day was sunny with a cool breeze. Some years March, April, and May can have perfect weather. I would like to go back again and take some photos and maybe a video. There were turtles, birds, and lots of trees that don't belong in Virginia. I spoke with a pretty woman who worked there as a childrens educator. She talked about Boxerwood and showed me where the crypt was located. As is my habit, and too often my downfall, I fall in love with just about every woman I meet. What's up with that? And, yes, some of the Munger family are buried on the site. Further, there is an area at the back of Boxerwood with a plaque describing interments. Apparently, future "natural" burials (you know, like a body farm, perhaps not that extreme) are planned in that location. It seemed out of place and a bit creepy. An overall impression that I got was a place with Wiccan/witch overtones. Maybe that is just due to my experiences with wiccan girlfriends over the years. For me, it just made the place all the more interesting. And if you are trying to elude evil spirits then there's a zig zag bridge, which may or may not help.

Things Have Changed

Just about finished with my first deliberate fast. It is only a 36 hour fast, just to see how the old body does. I dropped a pound, so far, which isn't the whole point of fasting really. It seems that a pound a day is typical, well, at first anyways. Our bodies seem to do at least one thing very well, and that is adapt to changes. As if there is some built in mechanism to seek equilibrium (as in stasis).

I have gone without food before, just not with a purpose in mind. Twice in my life I have gone seven or more days without food. Once was a silly bet with a friend, but I passed out on the seventh day after standing up too fast from sitting on the toilet. Let's face it, the very last place you want your body to be found is near a toilet. Especially if you didn't get the chance to flush. The second time I went without food was hiking the Appalachian Trail. I made it eight days without anything but water. One time I drank unfilter puddle water, now that's thirsty and a bit humbling. It was difficult, and due to bad planning on my part, and it was the longest period of time while hiking that I didn't encounter another person. Just my luck, no guardian angel appeared. Of course, after doing some research, lot's of people fast for longer periods of time. So I guess my experience was not such a big deal after all, but it sure felt like it at the time.

Why am I fasting? Well, I have diabetes type 2 and I am still a bit over weight. My hope is that fasting will help to control insulin and lose some weight. Time will tell. After all of these years I am only now learning to listen to my body. Sure, we all age, and we all break. My personal theory is that all of us only get so many heart beats. I just want to feel better and lessen my food obsession while enjoying my remaining heart beats.

All I can do is be me, whoever that is

rants

Why are there so many websites where the meat of the page is totally surrounded by ad's, tiny videos, follow us, and so on? We actually came to their page to read the article, and not to be bombarded by ad's, pop-up's, or whatever. It gets worse as you scroll down while reading -- pop up's occur or videos start playing or the page refreshes parts of itself and becomes unresponsive. It is so annoying! Wordpress is absolutely evil. My usual reaction is to immediately close the page. There should be a web browser setting that can only load the readable text on a page and nothing else. You know, like the old days of the internet. Ok, I found Textise which is slow but makes annoying websites more readable.

All the truth in the world adds up to one big lie
Bob Dylan

The biggest health problem is time. By that I mean everything happens so slowly as to be almost imperceptable. We humans suck at observing the small and slow changes that occur to our bodies. We seem to only notice things at the end -- like noticing you are over 40 years old when suddenly you need reading glasses. It always seems to be a sudden instead of a gradual event.
