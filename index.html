mandela effect Friday, April 7, 2017

Just watched the video Explaining the Mandela Effect (Prepare to have your brain scrambled!) by The Paranormal Scholar.  As always her presentation is cool and interesting, but it did not scramble my brain.  Often it seems the obvious gets overlooked in things like this, which is the fact that people are involved.  In other words, the common bit is the human condition.  So is it really all that surprising that similar things happen to different people, afterall we are not that different from each other.  My I and your I are almost exactly the same.  The skeptic flag is also raised by the appearance of any reference to psychology--Carl Jung.  Also, their website really sucks because WordPress sucks, pop ups that force me to click suck! 

The description for this video contains a much better motto for their channel: Discussion is the lifeblood of understanding than: The more you know, the more there is to fear.  I know, way too much complaining. 

following Friday, April 7, 2017

There's something about the word following that doesn't sit well with me.  Yet, I started following The Paranormal Scholar on YouTube.  Mostly I watch their history documentaries and not the paranormal garbage.  I don't like their motto: The more you know, the more there is to fear.  I get they are just trying to increase subscribers/views by being dramatic.  But that doesn't make any sense, as who in their right mind would want more fear.  Perhaps, they're targeting those into horror movies.  However, their history videos are well researched and lack the overly dramatic stuff.  Of course, this dichotemy between history and paranormal makes me doubt their content on the whole, but it's just entertainment afterall.  Their own conflict is apparent in the channel title: paranormal and scholar.  For learning about history I tend to view things like The Other Side of History, of course this is just as limited by the known as anything else--i.e. just because it has a college professor that is lecturing does not make it's content totally accurate. 

The reason I don't like the word paranormal is that I feel that anything we can experience is actually normal.  Isn't seeing, hearing, tasting, smelling, and feeling all normal things for people to do?  Assuming one can do those things.  The word paranormal just has too many negative connotations, whether it deserves them or not. 

trying to not think Thursday, April 6, 2017

Today is one of those days where I do not want to think about anything.  Have you ever tired to do that?  It is not easy to do.  Many years ago when I ran six or miles everyday, it was much easier to reach a point where my mind would just get quiet.  I don't know if that was because of all of those chemicals released when a body is under stress.  Meditation just never worked for me.  So now I'm stuck with just going for long walks, which only kind of quiets my mind but mostly I seem to think about everything: past, present, and future.  Mind chatter is just a natural thing, like digesting food.  Sometimes it works to completely exhaust myself by not sleeping enough, and evetually it seems to catch up with me and I completely pass out.  It is a healthy thing if you can stop thinking even for brief moments.  A friend had the idea that there should exist places, like beautiful parks or gardens, where one can just go and somehow while you are in there you can't think about anything.  It's a great idea, but how to achieve that somehow is the mystery. 

do it again Thursday, April 6, 2017

Do it again is something often said by children and implied by animals/pets.  And if you think about it, in a way, that simple phrase holds the meaning to why everything exists.  Could it be that what we call life and death is little more than the desire to do it again?  As in all over again, through fresh new eyes, so to speak.  To marvel at and play amongst everything that exists around us.  To experience everything.  Yes, even pain and suffering, although I doubt anything that has feelings would ever say do it again.  But pain, suffering, and other unpleasant stuff are a part of existence and just another experience.  Don't get me wrong, as I am in no way belittling pain and suffering because I have struggled with both of these for many years now.  Daily I struggle to cope with physical chronic knee, back, and stomach pain.  Given my past, I also struggle with mental pain.  Both together equal quite a bit of suffering. 

over population Tuesday, April 4, 2017

I have heard the everyone fits in Texas myth repeated a lot in blogs, NPR, and videos.  In case you don't know, the Texas myth is that Earth is not really over populated because everyone could fit in an area the size of the state of Texas.  Assuming we could tolerate being that close to each other, personally, I think we are too close to each other as it is now.  Here's an old school internet style web page that goes into further details. 
For those of us who love nature, it is frustrating to watch as planet Earth is raped, pillaged, and plundered.  Then again, the Earth was here before humans and will be here long after we are gone.  It survived the dinosaurs consuming everything in sight, and will survive whatever we do as well.  Yet, it's still frustrating and sad to just standby and only watch it all go down the crapper.  Change is the only reliable thing, whether or not we think it's for the better or worse.

health  Wednesday, March 30, 2016

The two biggest health problems are chance and time.  By chance I mean one may be predisposed to certain illnesses, it's just the luck (bad luck) of the draw.  By time I mean everything happens so slowly as to be almost imperceptable.  We suck at observing the small and slow changes that occur to our bodies.  We seem to only notice things just after they have happened -- like noticing you are over 40 years old and suddenly you need reading glasses.  As a voracious reader that was a bad day. 
It always seems to be a sudden instead of a gradual event. 

Eating always beats Exercising

Never forget that you can not out exercise what you eat.  Well, you can, if you try hard enough, but you will die trying.  After all, a handful of nuts (about 15) is the equivalent of running a mile or walking two miles.  You can literally chew and swallow 15 nuts in a few seconds, but you will be walking that off for 45 minutes. 

When it comes to calories, life is not fair.  Unless you have a math jones, stop counting so much and focus on the quality of what you eat and eat less of it. 

Never turn to the dark side

Do not eat food that's comes in a bag, box, or can -- most of it is not really food. 
There's probably a good, as in bad, reason it is stored in the dark. 

If it grew under ground (dark) then there are way too many carbs in it, so don't eat it.  For example -- carrots, potatoes, and peanuts -- these are roots and one of their main purpose is to be a dense energy store for plants.  Roots are the beer belly of a plant, and if you eat too many you will have a beer belly too. 

Respect yourself

Eating more than 30 grams of carbohydrates is not necessary.  In fact, we do not need to eat any carbohydrates, but fruit is nice sometimes.  Our bodies make more than enough sugars (carbs) already, often times, too much. 

Treat food like an intimate relationship, and don't just allow anything inside of you.  Respect your mouth as much as your other orifices. 

Let food be thy medicine and medicine be thy food.
Hippocrates
