Writing  Wednesday, February 14, 2018

Why do I write in journals so much?  Why do I buy so many journals and then fill them with words?  Am I a writer?  No!  Why?  Because I don't approach writing as a craft, nor do I wish to write a book or anything that gets published.  To be honest, I have found that writing so much makes me feel sad, not depressed, just sad from possibly too much introspection. 

There is a recurring dream where I write an autobiography, but not as a story that's interesting to read rather just a series of essays based on each year of my life.  The first essay would be the year 1955, and so on, and obviously the first five or six years would be hearsay since I don't actually remember much from those years.  Why do this?  Perhaps looking back on one's life is just something everyone wants to do as they face death.

Looking back on 2017  Saturday, January 6, 2018

Starting in June 2017 I began to add some running to my daily walking.  At this point, I can run 4 to 5 miles everyday as well as some weight lifting and lots of stretching.  My core, stomach area, is much stronger and flatter ... muscles are visible again, at long last. 

From May to December 2017 I collected a lot of oracle and tarot decks.  To be honest, my overall feeling is one of being disappointed.  The artwork on many of the cards is definitely not worth the cost.  The struggles with shipping delays/losses from Amazon, Etsy, eBay, and individual creators was a huge disappointment.  That was not always the case, but it happened a lot more than I expected.  So, again, I was disappointed.  In the end, I did not spend all of the money I had set aside for buying new decks ... basically, I became bored with the whole process of collecting decks.  In a way I will miss the "a package is coming" experience, as others have said, but I will get over that.

more crystal conflict Saturday, May 6, 2017

Even though I am very conflicted in my mind about buying crystals, several times I have made road trips to Jim Coleman Crystal Mines and Rock Shop in Arkansas.  If you resonant with quartz crystals then this is the place to touch and feel them.  It's amazing.  It's also extremely expensive.  Their website is old school, slow, and it's odd how they use both a tape measure and a Coca Cola can to give you an idea about the size of the crystal.  While I have purchased crystals online, it really doesn't make sense to do that.  After all, crystals are about feel, so if you can't touch them you just can't tell much about them from a photo.  Sure, not everyone in the world is blessed enough to be able to drive to some geniune crystal mine or store.  So you're stuck with ordering online, which is ok.  But I am still conflicted about owning them, yet it's become a habit I can't break. 

crystal conflict Wednesday, May 3, 2017

Over the years I have collected many crystals.  Especially quartz, clear and otherwise.  However, I often feel conflicted about buying and owning crystals that have been mined from mother earth.  The vast majority of crystals have been mined in a greedy manner.  And mining from the earth's perspective is rape.  After all, crystals are not a necessity for humans--we don't eat them.  So why do so many people, including me, collect them?  We're human.  We rape, pilage, and plunder.  It's what we do.  Sad. 

The following video puts a positive spin on the mining that occurs in Spruce Pine, North Carolina.  I was born and raised in North Carolina, about two hours away from Spruce Pine.  Just look at how damaging the mining process is to those beautiful mountains.  The narrator just casually mentions all the acids and processing involved in extracting some of the world's most pure quartz.  I am not judging nor trying to offend the person that made this video, in any way, instead it is enlightening to see the effects of mineral and crystal mining and I am thankful for this video.  One can only imagine how much damage is done in a more remote location like Brazil.  Hence my reason for being conflicted about crystals.  Another reason I feel conflicted, especially about quartz, is that without the mining of quartz in places like Spruce Pine I would not have had a career in computer programming.  No silicon, no computers, it's that simple.  For more information see this article.


insomnia Thursday, April 27, 2017

Insomnia has been a problem my entire life.  Usually I try to fall asleep while watching TV.  Some say that's why you have insomnia, but for many years I have tried falling asleep in a totally quiet bedroom.  That doesn't work for me, as I would just lay there with a gazillion thoughts dancing through my mind.  I discovered that if I listen to someone narrating a particularly boring topic I will fall asleep.  Most of the narrators that help have British accents.  Or they speak slowly and deliberately.  These were hard to find on cable TV, also I cut the cord many years ago--you know, hundreds of channels and nothing on.  So now I have amazon prime TV and mostly watch youtube.  But the only videos I can find on youtube that make me fall asleep are about WWII, Hitler, and Stalin.  These don't have commercial ad's and usually the audio volume is consistent and not too loud.  So I fall asleep.  But the same ole same ole gets old, and variety is the spice of life, right? 

I decided to try to make some of my own.  It kind of works, but I don't have a British accent nor do I have any oration skills.  Yes, I have tried all of those videos that are intended to make one relax like: fires, beaches, rain storms, ASMR, and so on.  They don't work for me. 

However, while trying to do really long videos I noticed something.  If I lower the resolution(720) and frames per second(30) on a GoPro Hero4 Silver and compress them using Handbrake, the file sizes are tiny and the upload to youtube is really fast.  For example, a 2 hour video compressed down from 20GB to 400MB and uploaded in about 12 minutes.  A huge difference.  Yet, they still look pretty decent, not HD, but not unwatchable.

falling Friday, April 14, 2017

Someone, maybe Einstein, once said that gravity is nothing more than a manifestation due to everything falling towards something bigger.  The whole space-time distorted idea.  It's a bit like this: put a bowling ball on a mattress, then roll another ball towards it, that ball will fall into the bowling ball.  It's not being pulled by some force emanating from the bowling ball, rather it falls into it due to a curvature (distortion) in the mattress.  But most of us see gravity as something that pulls things in and not as falling towards something.  That's fine, but then there are magnets.  Ever play with those?  Sure seems like there's some pulling going on with those.  Perception?  Being human is so limiting.

health  Wednesday, March 30, 2016

The two biggest health problems are chance and time.  By chance I mean one may be predisposed to certain illnesses, it's just the luck (bad luck) of the draw.  By time I mean everything happens so slowly as to be almost imperceptable.  We suck at observing the small and slow changes that occur to our bodies.  We seem to only notice things just after they have happened -- like noticing you are over 40 years old and suddenly you need reading glasses.  As a voracious reader that was a bad day. 
It always seems to be a sudden instead of a gradual event. 

Eating always beats Exercising

Never forget that you can not out exercise what you eat.  Well, you can, if you try hard enough, but you will die trying.  After all, a handful of nuts (about 15) is the equivalent of running a mile or walking two miles.  You can literally chew and swallow 15 nuts in a few seconds, but you will be walking that off for 45 minutes. 

When it comes to calories, life is not fair.  Unless you have a math jones, stop counting so much and focus on the quality of what you eat and eat less of it. 

Never turn to the dark side

Do not eat food that's comes in a bag, box, or can -- most of it is not really food. 
There's probably a good, as in bad, reason it is stored in the dark. 

If it grew under ground (dark) then there are way too many carbs in it, so don't eat it.  For example -- carrots, potatoes, and peanuts -- these are roots and one of their main purpose is to be a dense energy store for plants.  Roots are the beer belly of a plant, and if you eat too many you will have a beer belly too. 

Respect yourself

Eating more than 30 grams of carbohydrates is not necessary.  In fact, we do not need to eat any carbohydrates, but fruit is nice sometimes.  Our bodies make more than enough sugars (carbs) already, often times, too much. 

Treat food like an intimate relationship, and don't just allow anything inside of you.  Respect your mouth as much as your other orifices. 

Let food be thy medicine and medicine be thy food.
Hippocrates
That's a nice quote, but don't forget that Hippocrates was a male in ancient Greece.
Which means he thought women were lesser humans, as in suffering from hysteria and a wandering uterus.
And so, take his advice with a lot of salt.
