Hardware Experience

mainframe:
every IBM 360, 370, etc. -- basically everything IBM has made starting with the 360 and beyond

can you imagine:
- disk drives and tape silos that are across the room from the cpu -- now that's speed and storage
- a special built data center with:
    - cables the size of your arm hidden under the floor
    - a temperature that's so cold you could hang meat on hooks
    - halon gas dispensers to stop fires (WTF? who needs an ozone layer)
Now that is serious computing with unlimited power, resources, and a budget for such. Entering that room, well, it felt a bit like meeting the source in the Matrix.

mini's:
DEC's PDP-11, Sun, Honeywell, Sperry Rand

PC's:
Altair, Super ELF, Timex Sinclair, Commodore's stuff, IBM, every Apple ever made, and a room full of self-built computers including large servers, raspberry pi's, banana pi's, arduino's -- you name it

All of them still work, and my house is sort of a museum (think, Addams Family).
And I am their curator, well, more like Lurch.
