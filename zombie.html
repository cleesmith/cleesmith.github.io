being a sleepwalker  Saturday, April 1, 2017

Ok, the link for this page may be considered clickbait, but I am a sleepwalker, which is zombie-like.  No, I don't walk around with my arms stretched out make growling noises and eating brains.  Wait, I have eaten pig brains, which are good with scrambled eggs despite the similarity in texture, along with a slice of grilled liver pudding.  Boy howdy, that's good eatin' there.  The hillbilly moment has passed.  Sorry.  Since I can't control the sleepwalking, it has always been a source of shame and embarrassment.  In college everyone in the dorms thought it was hilarious, as I was often found outside totally naked.  There was even a picture in the local newspaper, well, with my privates redacted.  Not exactly the kind of fame anyone is looking for, at least any girl looking out their dorm window got a good look at a future prospect.  My first wife, met her at college by the way, often said she preferred sex with my sleepwalking self as "that other guy was a better lover".  Funny, but maybe she wasn't kidding.  My second wife thought sleepwalking was very creepy, which seems to be the most common reaction. 

When I bought this minimalist house I added very loud alarms on the doors and windows to wake me.  Because one time I saw myself on an outdoor security camera holding a pistol and getting into the car.  Luckily I did not go anywhere.  But I seem more capable than the experts claim on the various websites I've read.  Also, the sleepwalking seems to have increased as I've gotten older, which is the opposite of what the experts say. 

I have installed many motion detection IR cameras in many rooms to record any sleepwalking activities.  I have hundreds of these videos.  It is weird to watch yourself doing things you don't remember.  There are two recurring behaviors:

  1. I send myself emails that always say something like:
        "i will never forgive you" but always misspelled like: "no 4give u", "n'er forgue yu", and so on
  2. I light candles and walk around while holding them, yet I never bump into anything.  But when I'm awake I'm always bumping into things.
I tried to share these on YouTube, but there were many nasty comments so I deleted them.  Plus it was a lot of work to edit and piece together in the proper order footage from several video cameras.  Perhaps this is something that shouldn't be shared with the world.  The first thing I do each morning is to watch any captured video footage.  It's my own private YouTube, except the "You" appears to be someone else in possession of my body.  The sleepwalking me is extremely fond of masturbating, which is not something I want to watch ever--much less the first thing in the morning.  WTF, will I never run out of gism?

Occasionally, I will chain myself while in bed to avoid doing something horrible, like taking a gun and shooting someone or driving the car without being awake.  Actually, the chain is a synthetic strap that loops through an anchor bolt in the floor, and I am handcuffed to that -- sounds kinky, huh?  No, it's not my own private fifty shades of whatever.  Since I keep a pistol under my pillow (who doesn't?), there's always a chance that my sleepwalking self will kill me.  Is that murder or suicide or both?  Either way, I'm fully prepared to testify under oath that he did it.
