People Who Don't Know They're Dead: How They Attach Themselves to Unsuspecting Bystanders and What to Do About It

by Gary Leon Hill
☠☠  June 2007  (reread April 2017)
This is more of an embellished folk story than a true account of actual events.  It offers very little information about the dead and spirits.  But it does have a useful Bibliography and Suggested Reading section.  For my taste it is way too religious.  Some of the longer middle chapters of the book are interesing, as they discuss hitchhiking spirits (similar to possession).  Unfortunately, the book refers to Uri Geller as though his claimed abilities were a fact, yet fails to mention that he was debunked on the Johnny Carson show with the help of James Randi.  However, if you are just curious about death this isn't a bad book to read, but a better choice would be Thirty Years Among the Dead which can be freely downloaded. 
Excerpts:
"... we've learned that being dead is so much like being alive that many people have died without realizing their condition."
I believe that, especially if their death was sudden and tramatic

 

Dark Matters: Exploring the Dark End of the Magical Spectrum

by Samantha Valens  :    website
☠☠☠☠☠  January 2017
The author starts off by sharing some tidbits from her life and points out why some of this has attuned her to the darkness.  Although she does stress the importance of maintaining a reasonable balance between the dark and the light.  Including a brief life bio adds a nice personal touch to the book, allowing us to get to know the author and how these events affect her magical practice and beliefs.  The book also stresses the importance of journaling, a common theme in her writings, with many suggestions for topics to write about.  Most of these exercises in writing are geared to get you thinking and put you in touch with your feelings. 
Topics covered include: blood magic, sex magic, book of shadows, dark side of the four elements, skull and bones, dark crystals, plants, animals, dreams, spirit work, necrospirituality, necromancy, cemeteries and graveyards, trusting your intuition, hexes and curses, ethics, and lots of suggestions and exercises. 
Overall, it is an excellent book especially if you are just beginning to explore the darker aspects of life and magick.

Dion Fortune's Book of the Dead

by Dion Fortune
☠☠  May 2006
While rereading Dion Fortune's Book of the Dead I found a few nuggets of useful information.  This book was first published in 1930 as Through the Gates of Death.  I'm rereading it because the language of the 1930's is difficult to read as it is laced with religious and other time period references.  For example, there are lots of references to God, Christ, angels as if these are facts.  Also, not many of us know what a coal-scuttle is anymore.  But reading around all of that, there is some interesting information such as her description of the process of dying. 
Excerpts:
"... constantly recalling him [deceased] to contact ..." is the "... worse evil that can possibly befall him."
so be respectful of the dead
"... it is the intuition that perceives them, and not the sight."
in my experience this is exactly how spirits are noticed
