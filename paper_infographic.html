PAPER WARS

A lifecycle analysis of the two dominant species in the library: Archival Hardcover vs. Mass Market Paperback.

1. The Chemistry of Decay

The primary difference between a book that lasts 50 years and one that lasts 500 lies in a single organic polymer: Lignin.

Lignin is the "glue" that holds trees upright. In Mass Market Paperbacks (Mechanical Pulp), this glue remains, reacting with oxygen to turn pages yellow and brittle. In Archival Hardcovers (Chemical Pulp), it is cooked away, leaving only pure, stable cellulose.

4. The Timeline of Degradation

Why does paper yellow?

It's acid hydrolysis. The lignin in cheap paper produces acids as it degrades. These acids break the cellulose chains, making the paper brittle and yellow.
