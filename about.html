    nowadays

    Once upon a time my life was utterly consumed with computers and programming them.  And some family time.  And making money.  It was 24 hours a day and 7 days a week, the sort of thing that wears you out and fills you with regret in retrospect.  Now that I am old enough and can afford to not work anymore, I am beginning to get familiar with doing nothing.  By nothing I mean absolutely nothing.  It has taken over a year to get comfortable doing nothing.  It is hard to just walk away from working and easy money.  I am still drawn to computers and programming.  But ever so slowly I am beginning to focus all of my time on my past, history, death, and spirituality. 

    When I was young I was exposed to the Catholic and Baptist churches.  As well as many Native American Cherokee beliefs, which are similar to paganism, witchcraft, and necromancy.  Of course, that's just my opinion.  I do not enjoy churches: the perfume, dressing up, and hypocrisy are all too much.  A lot of churches changed what used to be "fire and brimstone" style services to something called "praise and prayer" which involve dominant church members talking way too much.  I missed getting "damned and yelled at" then singing songs, so I stopped going.  Although, there are some aspects of Christianity that I do respect: "do unto others as you would have them do unto you" and other useful ideas. 

    However, for most of my life I think of myself as someone on a search for spirituality.  I have tried being christian, being pagan, being a witch, being a necromancer, being whatever--you name it.  To me, enlightenment isn't possible.  Knowing the meaning of life isn't possible.  I have come to the conclusion that this thing we call life is just a collection of separate organisms that are somehow stuck together to form a human (or animal or plant or whatever).  That collection of separate organisms seems to want to stay alive and procreate -- big time, they cling to life.  As a human we in control of less than 1 percent of the organisms (cells) that make up our body.  When we are asleep we are in control of nothing, some people even pee and poop in their sleep.  Some of us are plagued by sleepwalking.  Occasionally, I even chain myself while in bed to avoid doing something horrible, like taking a gun and shooting someone or driving the car without being awake.  Actually, the chain is a synthetic strap that loops through an anchor bolt in the floor, and I am handcuffed to that -- sounds kinky, huh?  Nope, it's not fifty shades of anything. 

    I do believe (as in a belief not a certainty) there is more to life than just a body.  Call it soul, spirit, or whatever.  All bodies decay back into star dust.  It sounds better to say "star dust" than mud or dust or a pile of chemicals (rocks). 

    I have felt or sensed it.  Whatever it is, I believe it is. 

    I believe we may never know the answers to all of our questions.  Maybe there are no answers or maybe the answers are right in front of our noses and we just can't understand.  Or perhaps knowing is way overrated, and it's just a human nature sort of thing to always be in detective mode -- it's innate to try and figure stuff out.  We are curious in more ways than one.
