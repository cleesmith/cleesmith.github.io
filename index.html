13 signs Sunday, April 23, 2017

I watch youtube videos quite often.  Mainly because I cut the cable TV cord a few years ago, as it was just a waste of over $100 each month.  But also because there are some interesting and original content on youtube.  Also, I got Amazon Prime for the 2 day shipping but it includes some free movies, TV shows, and a youtube app.  So that is how I watch youtube, most of the time.  To be honest, most of the videos look much better on my 27 inch iMac and sound better with Sennheiser headphones.  Those headphones are so perfect: very light and soft so you can wear them for a long time, and they block out the noise of the hillbilly trucks driving by outside with their mufflers removed. 

I got off topic there.  My point was that I made a new video as a sort of response to all of those witchy questions/signs/topics that you see everyone doing.  Personally, I do not like any labels, and that includes weirdo, witch, pagan, cunning folk, or whatever.  Let's face it, given the vast majority of people that are not attuned to their spirituality and the natural world around us, labeling yourself with such words is an invitation to be burned at the stake.  Thankfully burning is no longer that popular.  It seems that was replaced by bullying and negativity, mostly, and those aren't so bad as being roasted alive.  I will probably also do a video with the witchy questions/signs/topics that one commonly sees, but for this video I created my own list of questions (or signs, as I called it).  A strange thing happened that was not done intentionally.  And that was that the list is 13 in length.  If you know HTML, what I used was the ol tag, which defines an ordered list.  In other words, it numbers the items in the list automatically.  All I did was sit down and type them off the top of my head.  And it turned out to be 13 -- that was a sign in and of itself -- good or bad is up to you. 


The 13 signs:
  1. Are you superstitious ?
    For example, you don't step on cracks in the sidewalk, would never break a mirror,
    step over lines between points while playing tennis, throw salt over you shoulder, and so on.
  2. Have you ever relied on intuition (gut feeling) when making a decision ?
  3. Do you quickly bond with animals that you encounter ?
  4. Do you kill insects, or do you respect their life and allow them to continue living ?
  5. Are you concerned about the Earth and it's environment ?
    Or do you think the Earth and universe were here long before people and will continue long after we are gone.
    And that any influence humans may have on any thing is miniscule at best, as we tend to think we are way more important than we actually are.   Thoughts ?
  6. Do you care about other people, or have empathy for them, even if you don't really like most people ?
  7. Do you believe in or have you ever experienced synchronicity (meaningful coincidences) ?
  8. Have you had flashes of insight that verified as true ?   ... these are different from intuition
  9. Do you find yourself just staring blankly at things ?
  10. Have you ever experienced deja vu ?   If so, describe it.
  11. Have you ever fallen asleep outdoors: under the stars, or in the sand on the shore of an ocean ?
    If yes, where and how did it make you feel ?
  12. Have you ever spent long periods of time outside in the dark, like at 2am on a pitch black night ?
    All alone and unarmed ?
    Did every sound frighten you ?
    Did you feel like something was watching you ?
    Or were you comfortable and at peace.
    Do you do this often ?
  13. Do you feel drawn to cemeteries or hospitals ?   If so, why ?

falling Friday, April 14, 2017

Someone, maybe Einstein, once said that gravity is nothing more than a manifestation due to everything falling towards something bigger.  The whole space-time curvature idea.  It's a bit like this: put a bowling ball on a mattress, then roll another ball towards it, that ball will fall into the bowling ball.  It's not being pulled by some force emanating from the bowling ball, rather it falls into it due to a curvature in the mattress.  But most of us see gravity as something that pulls things in and not as falling towards something.  That's fine, but then there are magnets.  Ever play with those?  Sure seems like there's some pulling going on with those.  Perception?  Being human is so limiting.

Often people say that we are all selfish most of the time.  That's probably true.  How can we not be?  We are human.  However, for me, giving to others started back in the early 2000's after working a lucrative programming gig.  It was just plain luck that I got the gig instead of someone else.  So that unexpected wealth plus a promise to a friend got me started giving to others.  By others I mean random others.  I was also influenced by the books and movies: Pay It Forward and Nobody's Fool, even though each book and movie are very different.

At this point, the act of giving and the recepients have all become some vague distant random nothingness.  Sometimes they don't even say thank you.  I just feel an empty, detached feeling of nothing.  So why continue?  I suppose it's just something to do during retirement.

hide contact form email to avoid spam Saturday, April 8, 2017

Formspree is free for 1000 submissions per email each month.  Their service has been around for a while, so I'm giving it a try, so far so good.  I doubt I will ever get anywhere near 1000 emails per month, thankfully.  However, if one just follows the instructions on their website you will be exposing your email to spammers and bots that harvest emails.  So a better approach is to hide your email by encoding it as base64 and then using javascript to decode it for the contact's form action.  Sure, any human could just use some javascript to decode, but spammers rely on automation--otherwise that's not spamming.  Also, I do get some spam anyways as my email is listed on several social media sites.  What's next?  Maybe give Disqus a try for comments, as most people don't really like sending emails even through a contact form.  Then again, as I am not selling me or anything, building an audience is not really important to me.  Maybe not, as using Disqus probably means that ad's appear, which is something I find annoying.

over population Tuesday, April 4, 2017

I have heard the everyone fits in Texas myth repeated a lot in blogs, NPR, and videos.  In case you don't know, the Texas myth is that Earth is not really over populated because everyone could fit in an area the size of the state of Texas.  Assuming we could tolerate being that close to each other, personally, I think we are too close to each other as it is now.  Here's an old school internet style web page that goes into further details. 
For those of us who love nature, it is frustrating to watch as planet Earth is raped, pillaged, and plundered.  Then again, the Earth was here before humans and will be here long after we are gone.  It survived the dinosaurs consuming everything in sight, and will survive whatever we do as well.  Yet, it's still frustrating and sad to just standby and only watch it all go down the crapper.  Change is the only reliable thing, whether or not we think it's for the better or worse. 

rant about blogs Saturday, April 1, 2017

Why do people have text in their blogs that looks like it does in printed books?  By that I mean why is the right hand margin all even and lined up ... it looks weird.  Does anyone space out words to even up the right hand margin when they write by hand.  No!  It just looks weird and unnatural.  Yes, I get why they do it in books, to save on the cost of paper.  But it still looks weird and hyphenated words are annoying to read.

rant about websites

It is so damn annoying!

Why are there so many websites where the meat of the page is totally surrounded by ad's, tiny videos, follow us, and other annoying garbage? I guess the "word" is not as powerful as we've been told.  We actually came to their page to read the article, and not to be bombarded by ad's, pop-up's, or whatever.  It gets worse as you scroll down while reading -- pop up's occur or videos start playing or the page refreshes parts of itself and becomes unresponsive.  My usual reaction is to immediately close the page.  Yes, I will not follow, nor like, nor participate in such annoying nonsense -- usually the content on such websites is totally useless.  There should be a web browser setting that can only load the readable text on a page and nothing else.  You know, like the old days of the internet.  Ok, I found Textise which is slow but makes annoying websites more readable.

health  Wednesday, March 30, 2016

The two biggest health problems are chance and time.  By chance I mean one may be predisposed to certain illnesses, it's just the luck (bad luck) of the draw.  By time I mean everything happens so slowly as to be almost imperceptable.  We suck at observing the small and slow changes that occur to our bodies.  We seem to only notice things just after they have happened -- like noticing you are over 40 years old and suddenly you need reading glasses.  As a voracious reader that was a bad day. 
It always seems to be a sudden instead of a gradual event. 

Eating always beats Exercising

Never forget that you can not out exercise what you eat.  Well, you can, if you try hard enough, but you will die trying.  After all, a handful of nuts (about 15) is the equivalent of running a mile or walking two miles.  You can literally chew and swallow 15 nuts in a few seconds, but you will be walking that off for 45 minutes. 

When it comes to calories, life is not fair.  Unless you have a math jones, stop counting so much and focus on the quality of what you eat and eat less of it. 

Never turn to the dark side

Do not eat food that's comes in a bag, box, or can -- most of it is not really food. 
There's probably a good, as in bad, reason it is stored in the dark. 

If it grew under ground (dark) then there are way too many carbs in it, so don't eat it.  For example -- carrots, potatoes, and peanuts -- these are roots and one of their main purpose is to be a dense energy store for plants.  Roots are the beer belly of a plant, and if you eat too many you will have a beer belly too. 

Respect yourself

Eating more than 30 grams of carbohydrates is not necessary.  In fact, we do not need to eat any carbohydrates, but fruit is nice sometimes.  Our bodies make more than enough sugars (carbs) already, often times, too much. 

Treat food like an intimate relationship, and don't just allow anything inside of you.  Respect your mouth as much as your other orifices. 

Let food be thy medicine and medicine be thy food.
Hippocrates
