hospital  Thursday, March 30, 2017

I spent yesterday evening in the car parked just outside a nearby hospital. I have been rereading a book I retrieved from storage: People Who Don't Know They're Dead: How They Attach Themselves to Unsuspecting Bystanders and What to Do About It by  Gary Leon Hill. Unfortunately, I have never had any coherent conversations like described in the book. Most of what I have experienced over many years has been feelings, intuitions, and emotions. Obviously there is a lot of death at hospitals, and some of those people are uncertain and confused about what has happened to them. In other words, they never prepared to die. Probably never even thought about death very much, like most people were too busy just living each day.
