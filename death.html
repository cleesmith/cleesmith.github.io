respectful

After rereading this page, it felt as though I was being disrespectful or trivializing death.
To be clear, I have the utmost respect for the dead and the process of dying.
These are just death related events that have occurred in my life.

tinker bell  Monday, April 10, 2017

Do you believe in fairies?  What I mean by that is do you remember the time when you stopped playing like a child.  I was able to find a comment I made in an old journal.  It didn't have an exact date and time, but it was more like I realized that I no longer played with my G.I. Joe doll.  Wait, I was a boy so Joe was an action figure and not a doll, which is bullshit as Joe was as much a doll as Ken.
Why is this on the Death page?
Because death is just another big change in your life.  The same as the rite of passage between being a child and becoming an adult.  Once it hits you, there's really no going back.  I feel, the same is true for the death of our bodies.  So, just as you mostly didn't fear becoming an adult, you shouldn't fear dying all that much.  After all nobody has a choice. 

a dear friend passes  Monday, March 27, 2017

Last week a dear friend past away.  I miss her.  I will miss her crazy emails and text messages.  She was an internet friend, if that counts as friendship -- to me it's a bit like the old days where people expressed unrequited love by writing letters and sending them through the mail.  We only met in person twice for coffee.  Both times were a bit awkward, as is usually the case for me.  There's something about sitting in front of someone's animated face that can be off-putting.  I assisted with her funeral arrangements, as I had promised her I would do, because her big family disowned her many years ago.  Forgiveness can be a tough nut.  She dealt with cancer for many years, not really clinging to life but going with the flow. 

Liz, I love you and I'm sure it's a far better place where you are now.
