Preface: Stories From Tarot Imagery  [top]

Caveat emptor

Due diligence requires a warning: this book is not a tarot guidebook.

After all, our world nowadays seems filled with tarot books, readers, videos, blogs, websites, apps, and literally zillions of those folded booklets and leaflets crammed and wedged into most deck boxes (LWBs). There is no shortage of instructional or self-help guidance regarding the tarot. Therefore, my only intent with this book is to share new images and stories near each other.

Often, the words represent imaginary stories inspired by something I noticed within an image, and other times, the words are based on one of the many “tarot moments” in my life. So that is the premise of this book, straightforward, but hopefully, the result will be interesting.

However, with that said, guidance or insight happens to each of us differently. So if anything contained within this book is helpful or evokes a response in you, that is outstanding.

Now, with the warning given, a bit more backstory.

Happenstance

The year is 2022, or so says the Gregorian calendar, and, likely, that planet Earth and humanity do not need yet another tarot deck. Yet, there seem to be thousands of them, with more on the way almost daily. The occult of tarot has gone viral as it’s exposed to the cold light of day. So why did I do yet another one? Even worse, perhaps, why did I do yet another clone of the Rider Waite Smith (RWS) tarot deck? Honestly, I have no simple answer or idea why I did so many digital images. This all started as practice for doing digital collage art, but over time, many of the images seem to fit into what I would call a tarot.

Artisanal

The images came slowly into existence from June 2021 to March 2022. Almost a daily morning ritual. A process fueled by steaming cups of black coffee (a blonde roasted siren), an Apple iPad tablet with the Procreate app serving as many canvases, an Apple Pencil serving as many brushes/pens/tools, an assortment of over 5,000 downloaded public domain images (via Google, Pixabay, Unsplash, to mention a few), and many hours of eye-hand coordination all while immersed in the colorful glow of pen upon the pad. The ability to use my eyes and hands in an artisan way was necessary, as I did not want to create images that resembled something cut-and-pasted using Photoshop.

Aside from artisan skills and beyond those cups of steamy sirens, a bittersweet muse lingered, perhaps Melpomene herself. Then again, maybe I just thought I saw something move. Enough said about the process.

Collaged

Why a deck of cards using digital collage art? In the past, along with others, I have seen collage art as a mixture of “high” and “low” art and not exactly “fine” art. My opinion has changed. After watching many documentary videos about collage artists and trying some analog and digital pieces, I now enjoy looking at many collage pieces. After all, can’t art be anything that expresses an idea or arouses an emotional response?

Ensconced

Personally, the images within an RWS tarot deck have always been a part of my life since birth even. As a child, I vaguely recall gnawing on several cards, teething on tarot, and later spending hours staring at those images. So I have always felt a close affinity with the tarot, and I am sure many others do, too. And with all the decks available nowadays, I continue to stare at new imagery that has grown beyond simple illustrations. Some decks have become small handheld works of art. Yet, ignoring my personal bias and certain traditions, I still see that the essence of tarot remains in most decks.

Flow

Not to bore you with all of these introductory words, but I should mention a bit about the layout of this book.

First, the book will display the entire tarot deck one card at a time with an image and a story: starting with the back image, then the Fool card, and so on until the King of Pentacles. The name of each image, i.e., a tarot card when it’s printed, matches a typical RWS deck of cards created by Pamela Colman Smith, Arthur Edward Waite, and the Rider Company about 1909. Of course, there are other styles of tarot: Marseille, Thoth, and maybe more. But most of my experience is with RWS, so that is what this deck embraces and resembles.

The name of each card is the same. The overall design and layout of objects within many images are the same. Any novelty within the deck emerges from the colors and objects selected for each image or is a byproduct of creating collage artworks digitally. But hopefully, the naming and design will make this deck familiar enough to be accessible to others.

Taropian Songs

The title “Taropian Songs” refers to stories inspired by an imaginary place that fully embraces the tarot. It’s a place I think of as Taropia, which in my mind’s eye, maybe a utopian, dystopian, or ecotopian society. Hence the “opian” part of the word Taropian with the “Taro” part being from tarocchi. Also, “opian” may refer to a name in Greek mythology, an actual place during medieval times, natural magick, or troubadours in France. Troubadours bring us to the word Songs in the title, and songs throughout history are often a way of remembering and sharing stories.

Ignoring the time sink caused by the infinite rabbit holes manifested via an internet search on any tarot topic, I find research, art, history, story, tarot, and song all fascinating. And fun! Now, at long last, we can turn these pages, stare at the images, and read the words.

It is my earnest hope that this book may bring many Taropian Songs to your heart.

Enjoy!

—Slip the Trap

Backs. From Behind:   [top]

Juniper’s dream.

Flat on my belly adrift down a canal in a subterranean cathedral, I can not move. Everything seems so strange and familiar too. So familiar, I am surrounded by stone, water, and animals, all clear to see. Strange, more so, as a lone figure with one giant eyeball instead of a head stands to my left. With both arms behind its back, striking a confident pose balanced by the water’s edge. Internally, I struggle against that maddening feeling of being unable to move my body; worse, I’m facing down on my stomach. I have always hated that feeling, as I recall being pinned down as a child by bullies. As well as those recurring nightmares of being trapped under the falling rocks of a cave-in. Yet, I am not panicked, just distracted by those feelings. So, stay here, focus, and pay attention to this now.

In the murky water up ahead, I see a frog with a butterfly on its head, a big fish, and many crystal-clear mirror-like reflections. Reflected in the water, a falling feather, two turtles, a lone figure, a path of stepping stones, and a fish-eyed view of the surrounding stone columns and walls. There are sigils, perhaps glyphs, carved into a single column. The entire scene reminds me of something I once read about in the Egyptian Book of the Dead. Perhaps my heart will soon be on the scale of Maat and weighed against that feather floating from overhead. Maybe behind that door, up ahead, awaits Anubis to record my ultimate fate, afterlife or no, as he scribbles upon papyrus. Possibly lurking nearby is Ammit, ready to pounce, sensing my heavy heart. Final verdict, it seemed, I bid a brief goodbye to my personal eternal afterlife. Truly the end!

Not yet. My end lay ahead. Whatever conveyance I drifted upon came to a stop beside the figure with the gigantic eye. Now, I could turn my head slightly to the left, gazing into that eye, appearing close up as if filled with the entire universe. For once, I felt seen and seen-through, no secrets tucked away safely inside me. A total revelation of myself, awkward yet calming. In that utter stillness, with nothing moving, the figure conveyed messages in a silent voice. What seemed like messages formed in my mind as fragments, like incomplete altruisms. Fragmented thoughts about worry, regret, and many other human concerns, with an evident emphasis on being in the Now, like nuggets of wisdom that I could not piece together into a coherent whole. The gigantic eye blinked.

The gigantic eye closed completely, disappearing, with the entire figure receding and fading into the wall. My head snapped back, staring forward again. The surroundings were growing brighter and brighter as I drifted closer to that door at the end of the canal. It opened wide. The light blinded me. The awareness and annoyance of being face down and unable to move had returned.

I hated that feeling so much, struggling to free myself. Finally, it dawned on me, again, yet another dream followed by sleep paralysis. It is hard to express what that feels like, aware that you are asleep and waking up, yet you can’t open your eyes or move. Trapped seemed like forever, but I freed myself and opened my eyes. Immediately I turned over, face up, a read on what just happened and what lay ahead. The sunlight flooded my bedroom, much too bright for sleepy eyes. Rubbing then opening both eyes, the first thing in my focus, yep, my beloved painting by John Henry Fuseli, titled The Nightmare. Was that painting a subliminal culprit?

I swung my feet and legs off the side of the bed onto the floor. Get grounded, go vertical, go now. I stood up and stretched my arms up, a moment of sunbeam basking. I turned towards the cheval mirror to see what condition my condition was in, overall looking worn, not rested, definitely behind the eight ball. Mentally I repeated, please be awake, please stay awake, please no headaches, coffee, please.

Hunger beckons; starving after a long night, must seek greasy food to wash down with many cups of black coffee, get dressed, find a diner, and do it now. Perhaps doing such things will help me forget the nightmare and move forward and onward, hoping to suppress those thoughts planted, like seeds, growing in my brain. All of that, and probably an entire day filled with more mundaneness, just to avoid those visions creeping softly back again. They always do.

0. Fool: Faithful Mirror:   [top]

Juniper (Junebug) Phalls

Juniper entered the room with a cautious step forward; broken glass cracking as it crunched underfoot, beneath lay mirror shards upon the stone floor as another antique cheval had fallen and shattered. Pausing, she counted twenty-one shards. Breathlessly weakened after that long descent of the winding tower staircase and the struggle to open those immense doors, she stood again in her most special place. She waited for her eyes to adjust to the darkness while trying to catch her breath.

Despite clutching the banister handrail for support, each step downward had quickened her pulse, inducing a loud tick tock because of her racing heart, an unheard but felt sound filling her ears and seemingly the entire room. The hypnotic rhythmic beats of her heart and breath mark time like a pendulum, reminding her of a scene from a solemn ethereal story by writers like Poe. Every visit to the room seemed to induce a trance-like state within her. The reason she returned to this room? Could the exertion of descending that staircase be the reason for causing her trances and visions? More unknowns and riddles puzzled her, yet here she stood again. Perhaps she had an addiction to questions with no answers.

Reminding herself to focus, breathe deeply, calm down, and steady herself before doing the cleanup chore ahead. Those twenty-one mirror shards require picking up, staring at deeply, then placing into a bucket with the many shards from the past. Mom told her that a broken mirror does not always bring bad luck. It depends on the number of shards, so she always counted them, each and every one. But for now, the tidying chore could wait a bit. She just needed to catch her breath and get her bearings, as visiting this room weakened her every time.

The tower and its internal staircase now remained only as an entrance to the room, once a great hall, which over time became an emergency keep for castle residents during raids, a place to hide. She thought the whole arrangement a clever bit of subterfuge to go down to a keep instead of the usual direction of high in a tower. For whatever reason, the room had slowly filled with some old furniture and tapestries. Still, mostly it contained mirrors of all kinds, with most of those being chevals. Why and how did all of this accumulate here? Why rows of mirrors, like pews in a church, as replacements for people who used to sit facing forward towards an altar? Often she felt watched when entering, not in a creepy way but with curious eyes, somehow welcomed and safe.

Except for the effort to get down here and the occasional chore of cleaning up broken mirror pieces, she loved this room and its contents, her favorite place in the castle. Her sanctuary. She loved feeling that sensory overload, a delirium, while wandering amongst the mirrors. They cast reflections, creating all shades of light and shadow, revealing or hiding this or that object. Inhaling deeply, the ever-present musty smell laced with wafts of old perfumes and woods accumulated from the past. Rummaging around, she would touch and rearrange things in her own private yard sale, so to speak, passed down by ancestors and never sold or shared. The room had once stood alone on a hillock as a great hall without a castle, just a simple circular building made from large stones and used as a dining hall or meetinghouse for the village. After that, a chapel nestled within a monastery and the origin of so many books she loved to read. And finally, used as a not-so-obvious keep for the castle owners to pray while hiding from invaders. Nowadays, keeps no longer exist, yet this one somehow survived and remained. Dwarfed by and entirely surrounded by an enormous castle, a lush green moss covers its exterior with ivies that climb up from the courtyard and its gardens. The room is an almost forgotten relic filled with ancestral treasures.

All the ancestors she knew or had heard of were women, the ladies of the castle, and all of them were Phalls. With every first name in honor of berries, shrubs, or trees; Willow, Ash, Momo, Hazel, Acacia, Olive, Rowan, Cerise (mother), and now Juniper. No family Bible, journals, or diaries ever had much to say about men, only sparse references to various invading Lords or roguish Vikings. So some good and some bad boys. But in all her reading, never once did anyone mention love, implying that each man visited, conquered, departed, never to return. Taking only what they needed, leaving the rest, and as best she could tell, only invaders of the heart. The details of any man existed only through a description of the daughters they had left behind; notes about eye color, hair, height, demeanor, and so on. Only mothers and daughters, all named Phalls, had ever lived in the castle. Juniper thought the line of Phalls ended with herself, as books contained her only knowledge of romantic love, or desire and lust, for that matter. She knew of no incoming invaders; no Lords nor Vikings loomed on her horizon. So no pillage, no plunder, and definitely no ravaging. Not a surprise to her, given her age and the local myths about the women of the Phalls castle; folk can remember men seen going in but never coming out. Not to mention all the usual myths that often surround anything strange. Strange, like women living alone while only giving birth to daughters, with the usual gossip about unnatural acts and witchcraft. But all she knew of life in the castle involved the reading of books, the utter and total quiet, the complete absence of men, and any romance for her only existed as a fantasy between the covers of a book.

Junebug, mom’s nickname for her, began cleaning up the broken mirror shards. Suddenly a petit mal seizure hit her, clouding her mind with the memories of her mother, Cerise. Time seemed to stop, entranced again, staring into the past, reliving it, unaware of the present. Then, as clear as can be, a memory of her mom came to life, appearing before her. Cerise spoke, yet again, about scrying in mirrors and how the Phalls’ women represent a long line of seers. She thought such abilities may bless or curse Junebug. Cerise bent over a shattered mirror and told her to pay close attention to each piece, especially if it had fallen under unknown circumstances while shattering upon stone. Then, she spouted some motherly advice about what she saw in the mirror shards that lay at their feet; three very foolish phantoms she called them: the Utter Fool, the Overly Cautious Fool, and the Bemused Fool.

Jumping up and out of one mirror shard, the Utter Fool; always leaping, never looking.

Half in and half out of one mirror shard, the Overly Cautious Fool; never commits fully to anything, always being, almost, but not quite here or anywhere.

Entirely out of one mirror shard looking dazed and confused, the Bemused Fool, forever entwined with sirens disguised as muses, always plotting the next big thing, never content for even a moment.

Cerise’s motherly advice about each fool served as an example of what Junebug should avoid becoming as she grows into an adult, and definitely avoid anyone with such qualities, most certainly men. Vision fading, Juniper saw Cerise grow quiet, staring, unmoving, as if in a cataleptic trance. Such trances occurred to both her and her mom over the years, petit mal seizures, a common occurrence in the Phall’s women.

Daydreaming of mom, she had cut her finger on a sliver of mirror glass. She sucked on it to stop the bleeding. Her finger left the taste of blood, iron, and dust on her tongue. Juniper, now fully aware again, brought back by blood, no longer immersed in remembering Cerise’s actions and words as the Junebug of her past. Her mom’s lessons often yielded no insight, meaning, or wisdom that she could understand. Her life seemed filled with ever more riddles to puzzle about.

The sun brightened the room inside the keep as a lone bird faintly sang outside. Its song entered through the narrow windows high in the dome ceiling. The bird’s song brightened the silence, yet the solemnity of this place remained palpable. Like herself, this place seemed so fragile yet unchanging, as if time itself stopped whenever she visited. A daily mystical ritual of some sort. She loved the early morning and the dark of night, each time creating a dreamlike state, like a night of delirium. Apparently, all the Phalls women took an odd sort of pride in experiencing entire nights of delirium. To her very core, these ritual visits always exhausted her, dredging thoughts about pursuing love and truth. This usually occurred when she stared as if scrying into those mirrors. They stood like sentinels, waiting, ready to illuminate something deeper yet never truly clear. Bringing past feelings to light, fuzzy emotions, causing teardrops to form in her eyes. It felt more like confessions welling up from deep inside, enlightening yet only revealing even more puzzles in her life. More questions to ask, more riddles to solve. She tried to stop thinking and moving, motionless, hoping time would follow her lead, but it did not. The flow of time resumed, as did the flow of tears in her eyes. Finally, the bird became silent, just her and her thoughts. Yielding to the moment, she cried, head in hands.

A teardrop fell to the floor. An audible splat upon the stone floor broke the silence. Bent over, wiping away tears, trying to see through blurry eyes, she knelt down on both knees to look closer. Peering into a drop of salty water, it formed yet another fallen mirror of her own making. Perhaps this one, formed by a natural act, revealed the truest reflection of herself. Yet, it only showed a solemn expression on her face. Yet, she believed her tears to be the most faithful mirror of them all, true objectivity, her truth within.

Pushing herself upright slowly, some melancholy lifted. Time to leave this place. She would tidy up the mirror shards on the next visit, time now to stave off her hunger pangs. She always wondered if anyone else in the world ever hungered after crying. She looked forward to wine and cheese after this emotional experience and the long climb back up five stories of the winding staircase. The regret of leaving filled her heart as she lifted the heavy skeleton key from around her neck. A long loop of braided red human hair served as a lanyard for the key, whose hair, which ancestor had sacrificed it, and why, she did not know. She struggled to close the enormous doors, locking them with the key.

Standing at the foot of the stairs, looking up through all those twists and turns, a dim light shone through a door she had left open. The staircase exited into the solar parlor, as her mom had called it, a topmost castle room bathed in natural light, another of her favorite spots serving her as a library with windows on all sides. She looked forward to picking a window, placing a soft cushion to curl up on while reading, or just absent-mindedly staring down on the castle grounds.

Grasping the banister rail, she never looked forward to this arduous climb, ever roundabout, ever upward towards where she spent most of her life. Back on the rails again, she thought, following whatever path lay ahead. She had slowly recovered from the state of delirium caused by entering the keep. To enter that room always affected her mind, causing her to look deep within as if those old stones held and replayed people’s memories, a true keep. At the same time, the mirrors cast light and shadow in the quasi-dark, setting the mood. Again, that old phrase popped into her head, and she whispered aloud, “as above, so below” continuing to pull herself upward. But, for now, she focused on what lay ahead; food, books, daylight.

Pausing halfway up the staircase, she resolved to stop returning to the keep and its dark shadows so often. Today, she simply must exit the castle and take a walk through the village, maybe say hello to some neighbors, and start doing it today and every day from this day forward. But, of course, every time she decided, she procrastinated, distracted by some book or a view out a window. She knew such a safe act, like a walk outdoors in the castle courtyard, required the willpower of steel. She must not always seek within but sometimes outside herself. Today, a leap of faith, for sure!

1. Magician: Nowhere To Now Here:   [top]

Juniper & Bjorn

Reaching the top of the staircase, Juniper felt dizzy from the long climb and that dance of resolutions in her mind. So many made, few of them kept, most faded away whenever she left the dreaminess of the keep far below in the courtyard.

That leap of faith resolution made only minutes ago had become a crawl. On all fours, she entered the passageway that led to a secret door at the back of her bedroom closet. Her mom had called the whole thing: the closet, the secret door, the passageway, the spiral staircase, and the keep itself; an “Oasis from Invaders of the Heart”–without any explanation. She pushed open the door to reveal twilight outside; time had fast-forwarded while down there; she needed a mood change, lamplights, or maybe candles. She stood and walked through her bedroom into the parlor library. She noticed a faint glow of light in the windows. Usually, the windows that faced the inner side of the castle, the courtyard, were dark like obsidian mirrors. Her face pressed against the glass to take a peek out. A circle of triangular-shaped tents stood covered in fairy lights in the courtyard beside the keep. Tunnel-like corridors connected each tent to a more giant tent in the center. A large man pushed through the flaps of the enormous center tent and leaned against his wooden staff as smoke encircled his head from puffs on a pipe. His head tilted back, and he looked directly into her window. Quickly, she stepped back.

The situation amazed her. She had not noticed their arrival, given the loudness of the chains and gears as the giant castle doors opened, plus there must have been a racket from trucking in and unloading everything. There must have been loud pounding needed to stake down those tents. It is quiet in the keep but not soundproof; her thoughts and visions must have created a full-on entrancement. Not to mention that she had not, nor ever planned to, authorize any visitors, so how were they here, now, out of nowhere? Long ago, she decided on an eccentric lifestyle, at least to outsiders, a recluse too, and to remain entirely in her own mind, so she did not want to deal with this new, unexpected situation.

Somehow these new folk entered right under the noses of the former castle servants and their children. They were now paid employees and made their homes in a semicircle around the castle. Altogether, they form a gated community outside the castle's old stone walls. An additional layer of chain-link fence enclosed their homes, monitored by security staff. No one had cell phones, as yet no nearby cell towers, so she used a walkie-talkie to call security. Juniper was told that Bjorn, an old Viking word for bear, had a signed and verified permission note. The guard said the note was ancient but checked out. Bjorn had even cited a little-known decree proclaimed long ago by her grandmother that allowed him, especially his ancestors, entrance to enter the castle grounds for entertainment purposes only.

She steeled her nerves; she would confront all of them and eliminate these carnies before they started the snake oil sales, so to speak. Now she stood before a giant sunburnt Viking with a red-orange ponytail atop his head and no other visible hair except a pointy braided beard. So not her type, she thought; wait, she doesn’t have a type or any prior experience whatsoever. He leaned on a tall Hawthorn wood staff, a dagger attached to a belt with a large buckle in the shape of a snake biting its tail. The image of the Magician card in her mom’s old tarot deck came to mind. On the back of his head, a bright blue pentacle appeared etched or burnt into the skin. Viking, perhaps not really, but he looked the part. These were her initial impressions of the man, leader of the troupe, he had proclaimed loudly and often. She wondered what effect on her impressions reading all those diaries of the Phalls' ancestors had somehow made her xenophobic. Is there a word for Viking-phobic? Bjorn immediately offered a deal; in exchange for a week or two of free encampment, he promised to leave no trace behind and not be a bother in any way. He offered daily free entertainment for anyone: comedy plays with some by Shakespeare, puppet shows, and exotic foods to sample, gymnastics, fire eaters, belly dancers, magic and magick acts, swallowers, escape artists, a psychic, a tarot reader, lectures about the human condition, and so on. It seemed a very long list for what appeared to be only about a dozen folk, some of those children. Juniper recognized what could only be called bombastic speech.

Yet, her steely nerves wavered in the face of all the delights offered. Bjorn seemed very confident in himself and the troupes’ abilities. Briefly, she had stuck to her guns. Everyone must go, she said to him, yet ever louder, he continued to speak, and the more she looked him up and down, she wavered, and curiosity won out. Finally, she shook his hand in agreement. Perhaps this, Bjorn and troupe, a sort of leap of faith to allow them to stay awhile. At long last, one of her many resolutions may become the truth. To not be so alone for two weeks, a good thing, well time will tell.

Bjorn began performances late that very night. They had erected a circus-like big top tent with torches lighting the way to the entrance while casting shadows around the lawn. Inside there were old-fashioned large drip candles placed on antique mirrored vanity tables. She loved the aesthetics. She sat in a chair beside Bjorn’s much larger and more ornate chair. They sat together in the perfect spot, at the center of it all, with an unobstructed view of the action.

Enthralled by the performances and Bjorn too; yet a tiny skeptical voice deep inside said, “calm down and take this slow.” But in the felt presence of the moment, she really enjoyed the excitement without a care in the world. So not another vicarious experience, like in the books she loved, but an authentic experience. It had been a long time since her mom had passed. Had her leap of faith moment arrived, somehow “out of nowhere” to “now here.” A dramatic change right down there in the courtyard, and that night she decided to allow them to stay for two weeks. She would tell Bjorn first thing tomorrow morning. The castle's neighbors, employees, and caretakers looked forward to a newness every day, and they seemed as excited as herself.

That first week seemed filled with an endless number of lavish entertainments. Juniper served old wines from the cellar, others brought delicious food to sample, and joy and delight filled every night. She enjoyed those extra moments with Bjorn more and more every day. They strolled about the castle and down into her secret place, the keep. He was kind, generous, and reliable; wait, could she be falling in love with this Viking? Had this been what her mom meant by invaders of the heart? Again, that tiny inner voice spoke a caveat emptor, but she ignored it and continued to have fun.

On the last night before Bjorn’s and his troupes’ departure, she went to the big tent as usual, but no one appeared, just her and him. In retrospect, she could clearly remember a toast to their successful and fun events and great times together. But then that night gets very fuzzy, very fast. Something had happened. The next day she felt dazed, like a hangover. In the mirror, there were bruises on her arms and legs. Stains on the bedsheets. Everyone had vanished, almost as if they had never even been there; no trash, and not even a blade of grass appeared altered. Perhaps the event had been an invasion, a modern-day version of a Viking pillage and plunder raid, without the berserker screams.

Although more time had to pass, she suspected that there may be another Phalls on the way. Bjorn had broken his promises; “to leave no trace behind and not be a bother in any way.” But, a child-to-be is definitely a trace, for sure, and might become a bother. Now, she thought, a storm of changes on the horizon; her life to be a lot less vicarious, becoming filled with all the reality she could handle.

2. High Priestess: Within:   [top]

Juniper & Rebekah & Leah

Perched on a parlor window ledge looking out, absently watching the raindrops fall, Juniper rubbed her belly. The storm of changes she had foreseen had indeed arrived. These rainy days of late seemed to echo how she felt; she was fuzzy and unable to focus. Then, surprise, not really, reality returned; she needed to pee again, another mini-flood.

Often she caught herself rubbing her belly while thinking of the number two. Somehow a two, her and Bjorn, had become a one, now just her, and ever so slowly becoming a two again. Such simple math, yet so much more complicated than that.

There had to be a better word for this state of being than pregnant. She felt like a fallen blimp from the sky into a vast ocean, immense and without direction. When she moved, it felt like being one of those weeble-wobble toys, mostly staying in the exact same spot.

Many had gone through pregnancy since the dawn of time, so of course, she could do it too, at least in her mind. There was no reason to play the "I am so special" or the “woe is me” card, which she had repeated so often, it had become a meaningless mantra. Yet the desire for some comfort and reassurance wouldn’t go away.

More alone than ever, she had to find a willing ear, anyone, someone who had been through all of this. So she reached out to a midwife, Rebekah, who had attended her birth. Her mom, Cerise, had always spoken highly of the Genesis family of midwives that lived near the castle.

Rebekah came to visit, bringing along her daughter Leah to chat about all things motherhood while snacking on cucumber finger sandwiches, sweets, and wine. Everyone sat in the old first-floor parlor near the front gates enjoying the art and antique furniture. Juniper could sense their excitement; a common thing for her, but a special treat for them. It seemed that Leah was the heir apparent to the position of the local midwife. Rebekah explained that nowadays, she could not continue at midwifery. She attended births only to share her counsel and wisdom and never again be hands-on during the birthing process. Those days were over, so I would be in the capable hands of Leah.

Juniper rubbed her belly, a new habit now, but as she looked up, both Leah and Rebekah headed over, kneeling beside her chair. They rubbed her belly too. She wondered what the baby thought about all of this rubbing. Then, unfortunately, she said aloud that she swore all of this rubbing had caused the baby to move or kick. Immediately Rebekah said that many moons must pass before the baby would move.

Before Juniper could say, “what does that mean?” Rebekah launched into a long rant filled with superstitions and old wives' tales. Too many to remember, but some of them went like so:

“there were precise moon patterns involved during pregnancy” (her belly sure felt like a moon),

“mixing urine with Drano can predict the baby’s gender”,

“full moons will cause orgasms or sexual fantasies beyond imagination” (a plus perhaps),

“some folk turned lycanthropic or werewolf-ish and more hair will grow; all over”,

and finally “never put your arms over up over your head as this causes the baby to get wrapped up in the umbilical cord”.

Such statements of fact spewed forth for many minutes.

Leah shot me a look, mom is crazy, and soon they said thanks for everything, then departed. Leah set a return date and told me to contact her any time of day if needed.

Briefly, their company had offered some comfort or normalcy. Still in a daze, as usual nowadays, Juniper ambled beside the bookshelves gliding the fingers of one hand along as she went, hoping a good book would present itself. However, one did stick out more than the others, stopping her fingers. It was a book she had never read, a rarity, the Gospel of Thomas. She was not really into reading about religion or theology. Still, some Phalls ancestors must have been, as this book was well-read and tattered. Holding it in her hands, it naturally opened to a bookmarked page. The bookmarker was a yellowing index card filled with scribbling.

Two sentences stood out as they had been highlighted and underlined:

“If you bring forth what is within you, what you bring forth will save you.

If you do not bring forth what is within you, what you do not bring forth will destroy you.”

All around the margins of the page were other scribbles; she recognized her mother's handwriting:

“It’s not about what you believe; it’s about how you experience reality.”

Another unknown scribbler added:

“... all of this is a mystical, psychologically modern idea that is heretical in that it suggests ‘you don’t need Jesus or the church even.’”

And in red ink, someone noted:

“... it means: deal with your sh*t or your sh*t will deal with you.

Be who you are or you won’t be able to ‘BE’.”

In all CAPS, someone shared:

“KNOW THYSELF!”

Judging from the date on each scribble this had been an ongoing discussion between the Phalls women of the past, albeit stretched out over time. She wondered if the discussion continued on, somehow, somewhere, perhaps in time she may have a scribble to add in that book.

Regardless of her bloated state and despite the long descent and seemingly even longer ascent of that spiral staircase; the time had come to return to the keep. Once more, before, it would be totally impossible to carry herself and her passenger down and back again. Reading those quotes from the book swirled about in her mind; definitely time to go deep within herself and spend some moments in that stone room that always seemed like her own personal oracle. Even if she often left the keep more puzzled than enlightened.

Juniper and her as yet unnamed passenger climbed back up the staircase, yet another resolution came to mind; she would take any practical help from Leah, ignoring most of Rebekah’s wives’ tales, and trust more in herself and learn to deal with the changes ahead.

This resolution might actually stick around because it was for two this time. Reaching the upper parlor, at last, and just in time as nature was calling. It was time to pee again; no puzzle there.

3. Empress: Wellspring  [top]

Any comfortable way of sitting no longer existed, so Juniper mostly stood, leaned, or walked until bedtime. All the while dreading a long restless night with the inevitable awakening and struggle to get out of bed. To face another day of not sitting down at all, just too tricky to get up, and forget about the joy of curling up with a book on a window ledge. No way, no how! A solution for coping with being pregnant did not seem to exist; only the cold comfort of knowing that countless women had been-here-done-this many times. And many of those women got through all of this with far fewer creature comforts than she had; again, not comforting to know.

With ever-growing girth, the best she could do meant leaning against her favorite window ledge. The sunlight on her skin felt great. Even a cloudy day offered light good enough for reading the old handwriting in the diaries of her ancestors. But all too often, she had to take flight about the castle; walkabouts on the same floor to relieve the aches. No stairs, no way! Short and aimless waddles, always the same direction, the same views, as if bone and muscle were squeaky wheels needing grease. She wondered if and what the baby inside her belly sensed. Landing [as in stopping to stand] back again to the same window ledge. An old diary lay propped open to the last page being read. She hoped that one of the countless mothers before her had jotted down any snippets of wisdom.

Looking down, Juniper recognized Rowan’s handwriting. Rowan was a great-great-something or other in a long line of Phall's ancestors. Rowan had been a writer and published author, not just barefoot, pregnant, and castle bound. Although Juniper had read a few books by Rowan, she could not recall much about them. Juniper read in search of pregnancy details and coping tips; just a little helpful advice, please. Rowan had written many diaries. Juniper’s investigation was bearing little fruit, other than suggestions to eat more pickles and ice cream until she happened upon the following diary entry:


Yet another day, 1796

Dearest RIP,

[yes, it seems Rowan often wrote as if these diary entries were letters to herself; perhaps a self-love thing back in the day; and RIP is short for Rowan Ivy Phalls]

The seed of the Swede ripens in my belly with each passing day.

Listen up, Dante, you missed the outermost ring of Hell!

Call it pregnancy; encompassing the other nine, all too familiar rings: treachery, fraud, violence, heresy, anger, greed, gluttony, lust, and limbo. I do not feel divine or part of a comedy.

The source of my discomfort hangs by a cord tethered inside me. Like a pendulum “IT” swings to and fro inside my belly. That’s not intended as a derogatory “IT,” but rather an “IT” in the absence of knowing whether “IT” will be a girl or boy.

I will never forget my time in this hellish ring; both sweet and sour. The sweet seemed to be that charming poet of a man named Sten. No, his charm uttered not from his mouth, not with his vocabulary; instead, his charm and poetry manifested in his body and movement. Oh, how I swooned, watching him, swaying about like an old willow in the wind. All too briefly, as his charms blew through this castle like a blast of cold winter wind then on his way to other haunts. What remained of him felt sour, often making my stomach turn. Of course, the fault is not “it’s” (boy or girl) but his and mine, too, of course.

If I could go back to when this pregnancy flared up, I would tell myself to walk more every day [Juniper smiled and whispered, comforted to know she was doing this already] as in don’t try to get up and down so often, eat just enough. And yes, cravings are natural, so give in to pickles and ice cream [Juniper looked forward to more ice cream later]. Be sure to drink water, stare at as many beautiful things as possible, and read daily. Oh yeah, never to let Viking men in my home again [oh yes!].

There are some things in life that words can not capture or express, yet here I stand at my favorite window ledge, trying to write how I feel down on paper. Often I dip the pen with only drips, drops, and tears appearing. With age, I felt I understood my mother’s motives better. I can not remember where I read this, nor why I remember it. Still, this quote often flashes into my mind: “Mother is the name for God on the lips and in the hearts of all children.” Which simultaneously sickens me with the burden of an awesome responsibility and blesses me with an opportunity to be a wellspring of love for a new arrival. A daughter, fingers crossed, or a son. Time will tell if I am up for the challenge ahead. It’s not like I can’t recall many things from my childhood to use as a guide, well, the good times, anyway.

Keep the barbarians from my gates; fingers crossed, and all will be well.

Love from a gravida (pregnant) and a pert homemaker

p.s. a diary really does help!

Juniper had found something; Rowan’s words rang true, even if only a faint echo in a dusty diary. She felt like Rowan was nearby, in the same room, with a hand on her shoulder and whispers in her ear. Shivers rippled up her spine. Now she knew she would make it through pregnancy. But, first things first, she would start a diary of her own to leave, perhaps for a future Phalls mother. Wishful thinking, but maybe the baby in her belly could sense her intentions as she wrote them down.
